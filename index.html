Common questions

Everything you need to know about AgendaBurn.

AgendaBurn takes each attendee's annual salary, converts it to a per-second rate, and multiplies by the elapsed meeting time. The total cost updates live every second as the timer runs — so everyone in the room can see exactly what the meeting is spending.
Yes — free forever for teams of up to 3 attendees with unlimited meetings. Pro starts at $4.99/month and unlocks Google Calendar sync, Slack integration, AI summaries, PDF export, and more.
Studies show a typical 1-hour meeting with 6 attendees costs between $300–$800 depending on seniority. Unnecessary meetings cost US businesses over $37 billion per year. AgendaBurn makes that number visible in real time, so your team can decide if a meeting is worth having before it runs over.
No. All salary data and meeting history are stored locally in your browser using localStorage — nothing is sent to our servers. Your data stays completely private on your own device.
Yes — both are Pro features. Google Calendar and Apple Calendar sync lets you load upcoming events directly into the timer with one click. Slack integration lets you post a full meeting summary — cost, duration, attendees, and AI action items — to any channel via webhook.
The most effective approaches: (1) Show the live cost to all attendees — visibility alone creates urgency. (2) Set a hard time limit with a visible timer. (3) Only invite essential people — removing one senior attendee can cut costs by 20%. (4) Replace status update meetings with async tools like Slack or Notion. AgendaBurn helps with all of these.
A useful benchmark: a meeting should generate value equal to at least 3× its cost. A 30-minute standup for 5 people at average salaries costs roughly $75–$150. If it doesn't produce clear decisions or alignment worth that amount, shorten it or cut it entirely.
